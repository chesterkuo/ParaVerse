Global Tech Sector Q4 2025 Sentiment Analysis Report
Prepared by: Meridian Capital Research | January 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The global technology sector experienced a turbulent Q4 2025, driven by regulatory crackdowns in the EU, a surprise rate hike by the Federal Reserve, and a wave of AI-related earnings surprises. Investor sentiment shifted dramatically across subsectors, with semiconductor stocks rallying 18% while social media platforms declined 12%.

KEY MARKET EVENTS

1. EU AI Regulation Act (October 15, 2025)
The European Parliament passed the comprehensive AI Regulation Act, imposing strict compliance requirements on companies deploying generative AI. Major affected companies include OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Meta AI, and Anthropic. The regulation requires algorithmic transparency, mandatory bias audits, and user consent frameworks. Share prices of AI-focused companies dropped an average of 8.3% in the week following the announcement. However, compliance-tech firms such as OneTrust and TrustArc saw gains of 15-22%.

2. Federal Reserve Rate Decision (November 3, 2025)
The Federal Reserve unexpectedly raised interest rates by 25 basis points to 5.75%, citing persistent inflation in the services sector. This surprised markets, which had priced in a hold. The Nasdaq Composite fell 3.2% on the day. Growth stocks were disproportionately affected, with high-P/E names like Palantir (-9.1%), Snowflake (-7.8%), and CrowdStrike (-6.4%) seeing the steepest declines. Value-oriented tech firms such as IBM (+2.1%) and Cisco (+1.8%) outperformed.

3. NVIDIA Earnings Beat (November 20, 2025)
NVIDIA reported Q3 FY2026 revenue of $42.5 billion, beating consensus estimates of $38.2 billion by 11%. Data center revenue surged 95% year-over-year, driven by Blackwell GPU demand. The stock rose 14% after hours. This catalyzed a broader semiconductor rally, with AMD (+8.2%), Broadcom (+6.7%), and TSMC (+5.1%) all gaining in sympathy.

4. TikTok US Ban Ruling (December 8, 2025)
The US Supreme Court upheld the TikTok ban, giving ByteDance 90 days to divest its US operations. This sent shockwaves through the social media sector. Meta Platforms rose 7.3% as investors anticipated market share gains. Snap Inc. gained 12.4%. Meanwhile, emerging social platforms like Bluesky and Mastodon saw user sign-up surges of 300-400%.
